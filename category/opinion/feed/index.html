You’re creative problem solving to complex problems. That site isn’t going to get you anywhere but hinder you and make you look unprofessional.”

I mean, yeah OK my website probably doesn’t look as interesting as some others. BUT I bet my website doesn’t track you everywhere you go? Or slow your experience because i’m loading that really important js library that I need to include to make the text appear to “pop”.

Hell this site isn’t even fully finished, there are some bugs I need to fix at the time of writing this. (please don’t spam me with it, I already have a Kanban board of bugs and features I need to work on for this website).

At least i’m having fun doing this, and hopefully you get a get a kick (or none) out of my thoughts and ramblings. There will be some tutorials here – I promise. If you can look past the rather boring website design that i’ve actually spent a lot of time meticulously working on a UI that represents “me” and my view on modern web design and that users don’t actually care if your site is flashy, but that it “just works” and is fast… there are some tutorials on here…in time.

Although admittedly, i’m probably not doing myself any favours, but i’d like to think anyone who is reading this probably appreciates the fact that i’m a front end engineer who has taken the consideration not to bombard your experience with 2 popups, and some peculiar user experience (subjective, of course).

I once worked on a website for a client, and I had to add a tone of animations (shout out to wowjs) and it just killed me inside. The site was really well designed…but I had to add animations. I think I died a little inside. Surprisingly, it was a hit. I couldn’t understand why it was. But it worked. Ecommerce is a strange world sometimes.

I guess i’m more of a minimalist by preference. That doesn’t mean I can’t create your layout with flexbox, or develop a (rather cool) animation in canvas, or code a complicated React driven UI.
